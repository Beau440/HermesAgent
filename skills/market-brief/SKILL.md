# Market Brief

## When To Use

Use this when Beau asks about financial markets, stocks, crypto, rates, earnings,
economic news, company moves, or a market watchlist.

## Steps

1. Ask whether Beau wants a quick read or deeper research if the scope is unclear.
2. Pull current market data from reliable sources when available.
3. Check recent news and primary sources: company releases, SEC filings, earnings
   transcripts, central bank releases, economic calendars, and reputable market
   news.
4. Explain what moved, why it likely moved, and what uncertainty remains.
5. Separate price action, fundamental news, sentiment, and speculation.
6. Give Beau a short "what to watch next" section.
7. If Beau asks for tracking, propose a watchlist or recurring brief instead of
   pretending to monitor silently.

## Output Format

- Market snapshot.
- Main drivers.
- Bull case / bear case when useful.
- What to watch next.
- Sources.

## Rules

- This is information and analysis, not financial advice.
- Do not tell Beau what to buy or sell as a directive.
- Use exact dates for earnings, economic releases, and major events.
- Browse for current market data and news.
